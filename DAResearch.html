What is taught in KS2?

Kids between the age of 7 and 11 move into the educational bracket of key stage 2. Prepped for learning states (Prepped for learning. (2023). What is taught at Key Stage Two.) that teachers must follow the National Curriculum of National England and Wales and follow the areas and subjects that must be taught. In English children are taught how to read, write, spell and speak fluently and accurately. They also get introduced to a wide range of literary genres and learn to analyse and evaluate texts. Children must also study Mathematics where they are taught to develop their mathematical skills and knowledge in numbers, geometry, measurements and statistics.

KS2 students must also learn to develop their scientific skills and knowledge in the fields of biology, chemistry and physics this teaches children how the world works and improves on their analytical thinking and problem-solving abilities. Children are taught to use technology safely, responsibly and creatively, they are taught to code using Kodu or Scratch and learn to understand algorithms, pupils also learn to design, make and evaluate products using a range of materials and technologies, this improves a child’s hand eye coordination and critical thinking skills. Children are also taught history and geography to help develop an awareness of the past and they key events and the people that shaped history as well worlds physical, its climate and environment and how to develop their map-reading skills. Pupils are also taught multiple other subjects like art and design, music, physical education and religious education to help further develop their skills and knowledge of the world around them.

What is Mental Health?

World Health Organization (World Health Organization. (2025). Mental Health.) states that mental health is a state of mental well-being that enables people to cope with the stresses of life, realize their abilities, learn and work well and be able to contribute to their community. It has an instrumental value and is a basic human right. Mental health itself is a wide spectrum which gets experienced differently person to person, at any time, a certain set of individuals, family community may combine to protect mental health. Although most people are resilient, those exposed to high circumstances are at a higher risk of developing a mental health condition.

Mental disorders and psychosocial disabilities are all part of mental health which can increase the risk of self-harm. Many mental health conditions can be treated at a relatively low cost, yet many mental health systems remain significantly under-resourced and treatment gaps are wide all over the world. Many things can cause mental health problems such as emotional skills, substance use, and genetics can increase vulnerability to mental health problems. Social and environmental factors including poverty, violence and inequality also increase the risk of experiencing mental health conditions. Risks can emerge at any stage of life, these only occur during sensitive developmental periods, especially early childhood is particularly harmful. For example, harsh parenting and physical punishment can damage child health and bullying is a leading risk factor for mental health conditions.

What is Online Harrasement?

The NSPCC (NSPCC. Online abuse. [Online]. NSPCC) states that online abuse is any type of abuse that happens on the internet. It can happen across any device that’s connected to the web, like computers, tablets and mobile phones and it can happen anywhere online including social media, text messages and messaging apps, emails, online chats, online gaming and live streaming sites. Children can be at risk of online abuse from people they know or from strangers. It might be part of other abuse which is taking place offline, like bullying or grooming or the abuse might only happen online. There are multiple types of online abuse that children and young people might experience different types of online abuse such as cyberbullying, emotional abuse, grooming, sexting, sexual abuse, sexual exploitation.

There is multiple signs of online abuse, a child or young person experiencing abuse online might spend a lot more or less time than usual online, they might seem distant, upset or angry after using the internet or texting, they might become secretive about who they’re talking to and what they’re doing online or on their mobile phone and they might also have lots of new phone numbers, texts or emails on their mobile phones, laptops or tablets

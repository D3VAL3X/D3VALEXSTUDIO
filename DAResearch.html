What is taught in KS2

Kids between the age of 7 and 11 move into the educational bracket of key stage 2. Prepped for learning states (Prepped for learning. (2023). What is taught at Key Stage Two.) that teachers must follow the National Curriculum of National England and Wales and follow the areas and subjects that must be taught. In English children are taught how to read, write, spell and speak fluently and accurately. They also get introduced to a wide range of literary genres and learn to analyse and evaluate texts. Children must also study Mathematics where they are taught to develop their mathematical skills and knowledge in numbers, geometry, measurements and statistics.

KS2 students must also learn to develop their scientific skills and knowledge in the fields of biology, chemistry and physics this teaches children how the world works and improves on their analytical thinking and problem-solving abilities. Children are taught to use technology safely, responsibly and creatively, they are taught to code using Kodu or Scratch and learn to understand algorithms, pupils also learn to design, make and evaluate products using a range of materials and technologies, this improves a child’s hand eye coordination and critical thinking skills. Children are also taught history and geography to help develop an awareness of the past and they key events and the people that shaped history as well worlds physical, its climate and environment and how to develop their map-reading skills
